### 5. Claude Desktop App Controversy: Hidden Native Messaging Bridge Raises Privacy Concerns

**Source:** The Register / Malwarebytes Blog  
**Author:** Alexander Hanff (researcher); The Register Staff  
**Date:** 2026-04-20  
**URL:** https://www.theregister.com/2026/04/20/anthropic_claude_desktop_spyware_allegation/

**Summary:** Privacy researcher Alexander Hanff revealed that Anthropic's Claude Desktop silently writes a Native Messaging bridge manifest into seven Chromium-based browsers — including Chrome, Brave, Edge, Arc, Vivaldi, and Opera — without any user consent dialog during installation or first launch. The bridge pre-authorizes a local executable called chrome-native-host, which operates outside the browser sandbox with full user-level privileges. Security experts debated the "spyware" label — while no active data exfiltration was confirmed, the undisclosed pre-positioning of system-level browser access drew significant developer criticism on Hacker News.
